    Week 17 — One-on-One Support & Feedback¶

    📅 Week 17 ⏱ 2 Hours 🟢 Phase 4 — Debrief 📋 ICTSAS305

    MISSION BRIEF

    Incident Reference: RBS-2025-017
    A Redback Systems staff member needs hands-on training on how to use their new hardened machine — specifically the password manager and MFA setup you recommended. You're delivering one-on-one IT support. After the session, they fill out a feedback form. You use that feedback to improve.

    Learning Objectives¶

    • Deliver one-on-one technical instruction to a non-technical user
    • Adapt your explanation in real time based on client understanding
    • Create and distribute a feedback form
    • Analyse feedback and identify specific improvement areas
    • Write a brief reflection on your own performance

    1. One-on-One Instruction Principles¶

    Pace to the learner, not to the content.

    Check in every few steps: "Does that make sense so far?" "Want me to show that again?"
    Don't keep going if they're lost — you'll lose them completely.

    Show, then watch them do it.

    Demonstrate once. Then ask them to do it themselves while you watch.
    Resist the urge to take over the mouse/keyboard.
    Their hands on the keyboard = actual learning.

    Normalise not knowing.

    "This is confusing for most people at first" is useful.
    "It's really simple" is useless (if they find it hard, they feel stupid).

    2. The Feedback Form¶

    You need evidence that you collected feedback (ICTSAS305 Element 3).

    # Redback Systems IT Support — Session Feedback

Date: _____________  Technician: _____________

1. Did the IT support person explain things clearly?
   [ ] Very clearly  [ ] Mostly clearly  [ ] Sometimes confusing  [ ] Hard to follow

2. Did you feel comfortable asking questions?
   [ ] Yes, very  [ ] Mostly  [ ] A little  [ ] No

3. Was the problem resolved?
   [ ] Fully resolved  [ ] Partially resolved  [ ] Not resolved

4. How would you rate the overall support experience?
   [ ] Excellent  [ ] Good  [ ] Fair  [ ] Poor

5. What could the IT support person do better? (optional)
   _______________________________________________

6. Any other comments?
   _______________________________________________

    Lab Task¶

    TASK 17.1 — One-on-One Support Session (AT1 Practical Task 10)

    In pairs — rotate roles (15 min each):

    Role A: IT technician
    Teach your partner how to:

    • Set up Bitwarden (free password manager) and generate a strong password
    • Enable MFA on a test account (your lecturer will set up a test account)
    • Identify a phishing email (use examples from Week 14)

    Use show-then-do. Don't touch their keyboard. Check understanding at each step.

    Role B: Non-technical client
    Play a realistic but cooperative client — not overly confused, not too easy. Ask one genuine question during the session.

    After each session:

    • Client fills out the feedback form (paper or digital)
    • Technician reads the feedback
    • 5-minute verbal debrief between you: what worked, what didn't

    TASK 17.2 — Feedback Analysis

    After both sessions:

    • Scan or photograph both completed feedback forms
    • Write a 150-word reflection: what the feedback told you, one thing you'll do differently

    Submit forms + reflection to AT1 portfolio.
